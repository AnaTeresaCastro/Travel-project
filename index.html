WHY PORTO?

Porto, the city of port wine

The legend has it that Lisbon was named Olissipo by the Romans, for its resemblance to the Roman capital. Just like Rome, Lisbon began its life as a humble settlement nestled among seven hills. However, it wasn't until the 17th century, in the writing of Frey Nicolao d'Oliveira, that Lisbon came to be called 'a cidade das sete colinas' - the city of seven hills.

CAFÉS

My favorite places in Porto

Manteigaria

When you combine the best raw materials with the mastery of artisanal production, you can only come up with something unique and delicious: Manteigaria's Pastel de Nata.

Address:

Rua Alexandre Braga nº 24

What I like about it

The best pastel de nata in Portugal! And they have nice coffee and sofas so it's a perfect place to enjoy a nice talk with a friend

LEARN MORE

Brasão

At Brasão Bistrô, they blend tradition with modernity, reinventing Portuguese cuisine in a refined beer house setting. Enjoy the best francesinhas, tapas, meats, and seafood, perfectly paired with sangria, cold beer, wines, and cocktails. A true Portuguese experience!

Address:

Rua Passos Manuel nº 205

What I like about it

The best vegan/ vegetarian francesinha! And the traditional francesinha is also the best. For desert, they have an amazing brownie

LEARN MORE

Padoca

Padoca is plant based, and you will be surprised when you try their food as it is absolutely delicious! From dishes, bakery, cafeteria , savory, to our delicious pastries and desserts

Address:

Rua de Pádua Correia nº 261

What I like about it

Everything there tastes amazing! from the pastries to the dishes or the starters
